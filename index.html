The Origin

I am a Bangladeshi high school researcher whose work sits at the collision of artificial intelligence, theoretical systems, and human resilience. Not a straight path — an unusual one.

From Khulna to global stages, I've built frameworks for ethical AI, authored papers on SSRN and Google Scholar, won the Queen's Commonwealth Gold beating 53,000+ writers across 56 nations, and shipped over 10 websites to the web — all before finishing secondary school.

My journey includes Lumiere Research Scholar mentorship with Harvard/Stanford/Oxford PhD mentors, a TED Summer School speaking scholarship, acceptance into Stanford Code in Place from 66,000+ applicants, and co-authoring a thermodynamics paper with a Research Assistant Professor from the University of Oklahoma.

I've faced profound personal challenges — schizophrenia, bipolar disorder, a life-threatening hospitalisation, the loss of my father in 2024. Each broke the floor beneath me. I kept walking. My psychiatrist Mst. Shammi Akhter believed in me when I struggled to believe in myself. My work on constraint-based systems is, in part, a love letter to the idea that structure can free you.
